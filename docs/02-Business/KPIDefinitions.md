# 1. Purpose

This document defines every Key Performance Indicator (KPI) generated by EPSM Manager.

KPIs transform operational production data into meaningful business information that supports business decisions.

Every KPI must answer at least one documented Business Question.

All KPIs are generated automatically by the Business Calculation Engine using operational production data.

Operators never calculate KPIs manually.

---

# 2. KPI Philosophy

EPSM Manager follows five principles.

1. KPIs are generated automatically.

2. KPIs are based on documented business calculations.

3. KPIs exist to support business decisions.

4. Every KPI must answer one or more Business Questions.

5. Business indicators must evolve without increasing operator workload.

---

# 3. KPI Categories

Current KPIs are grouped into the following business categories.

## Production

Measures overall production execution and operational compliance.

## Waste

Measures material utilization and production losses.

## Performance

Measures production performance across operators, recipes and shifts.

## Trends

Measures historical production evolution.

## Insights

Highlights operational improvement opportunities.

# 4. KPI Traceability

Every KPI must be fully traceable.

Business Question

↓

Business Rule

↓

Business Calculation

↓

KPI

↓

Dashboard / Reports

Business logic must never exist exclusively inside the software implementation.

---

# 5. KPI Lifecycle

Every KPI progresses through the following lifecycle.

## Approved

Business definition completed and validated.

## Planned

Business definition approved and scheduled for implementation.

## Future Ready

Supported by the current business model and system architecture, but intentionally hidden until the business requires it.

---

# 6. Design Principles

KPIs should always:

- Answer a real business question.
- Support operational decision-making.
- Be automatically generated.
- Be reproducible.
- Be historically comparable.
- Require no manual calculations.

KPIs should never:

- Require additional operator input.
- Duplicate existing indicators.
- Depend on dashboard implementation.
- Exist without a documented business purpose.
